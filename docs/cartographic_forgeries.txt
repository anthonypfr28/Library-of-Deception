EXHIBIT NOTES: MAPS THAT LIED
Archived by the Library of Deception, Wing III - Forged Objects

Cartographers have inserted fiction into maps for centuries, for
reasons ranging from copyright protection to national pride.

PAPER TOWNS
  Mapmakers invent a town that does not exist, so that if a rival
  map copies it, the theft is provable. Some paper towns were later
  colonized by mistake, becoming real because people believed the map.

SANDY ISLAND
  Charted in the Coral Sea for over a century and shown on marine
  charts and satellite-imagery services alike, until a 2012 survey
  found open ocean where the island was supposed to be.

PHANTOM MOUNTAINS
  Ranges reported by early explorers, sometimes real weather
  phenomena, sometimes icebergs, sometimes nothing at all, copied
  from atlas to atlas long after the "discoverer" left the region.

SOVIET DISTORTION
  For decades, official Soviet civilian maps deliberately shifted
  roads, rivers, and coordinates, a defensive deception intended to
  frustrate foreign targeting and unauthorized travel alike.

Archivist's note: a map is not a photograph of the world. It is an
argument about the world, and every argument can be made in bad faith.
