CASE FILE: THE CONFIDENCE GAME
Archived by the Library of Deception, Wing I - Grift & Ruse

Every confidence game, regardless of era or medium, follows the same
five-beat structure. The details change; the beats do not.

BEAT 1 - THE PUT-UP
  The mark is identified and researched. What do they want badly
  enough to lower their guard for?

BEAT 2 - THE PLAY
  Contact is made. It looks accidental. It is not.

BEAT 3 - THE ROPE
  Trust is built through small, verifiable truths. The con artist
  gives something real before asking for anything.

BEAT 4 - THE TALE
  The pitch. A story explaining why extraordinary reward requires
  the mark's participation, money, or silence, right now.

BEAT 5 - THE CONVINCER
  Early "proof" that the tale is working, usually a small early
  payout, designed to remove the mark's remaining doubt.

Archivist's note: the confidence game is named for what it exploits,
not what it grants. It runs on the mark's confidence, not the con
artist's. Suspicion is the only known countermeasure.
