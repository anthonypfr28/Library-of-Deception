DOCTRINE SUMMARY: THE GHOST ARMY
Archived by the Library of Deception, Wing IV - Strategic Illusion

In 1944, an Allied unit of roughly eleven hundred soldiers, many
drawn from art schools and design studios, was tasked with a single
mission: to be seen as a force many times its actual size.

INFLATABLE ARMOR
  Rubber tanks, inflated overnight and deflated by dawn, photographed
  well from reconnaissance altitude and convincing at a distance.

SONIC DECEPTION
  Recordings of tank columns, engine convoys, and bridge-building
  crews, played from concealed speakers audible for miles at night.

RADIO DECEPTION
  Operators impersonated the call signs and traffic patterns of real
  divisions stationed elsewhere, convincing enemy listening posts
  that units were present in places they had already left.

ATMOSPHERE
  Soldiers painted false unit insignia on their own trucks and wore
  the patches of the divisions they were impersonating, so a captured
  soldier or a curious local would tell the same false story.

Archivist's note: the unit's motto, unofficial and unrecorded, was
said to be "playing for keeps." Its greatest success is that its
existence remained classified for over forty years after the war.
