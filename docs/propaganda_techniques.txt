FIELD NOTES: SEVEN PROPAGANDA TECHNIQUES
Archived by the Library of Deception, Wing II - Persuasion & Power

1. NAME CALLING
   Attach a negative label to an idea or person so the audience rejects
   it without examining the evidence. The label does the arguing.

2. GLITTERING GENERALITIES
   The inverse of name calling. Wrap a proposal in warm, vague words
   ("freedom," "progress," "the people") so nobody asks what it means.

3. TRANSFER
   Borrow the authority or prestige of one thing and place it onto
   another. A flag behind a podium. A doctor's coat in an advertisement.

4. TESTIMONIAL
   Let a respected or beloved figure vouch for the claim, so the
   audience's trust in the person transfers to trust in the message.

5. PLAIN FOLKS
   The speaker insists they are "just like you" to disarm suspicion
   of their motives or expertise.

6. CARD STACKING
   Selective use of facts, half-truths, and distraction to build the
   most favorable case possible for an idea, person, or product.

7. BANDWAGON
   "Everyone is doing it." Invite the audience to join the winning
   side rather than evaluate the side on its own merits.

Note from the archivist: none of these techniques require a lie.
The most durable deceptions are built entirely from true statements,
arranged to imply something false.
